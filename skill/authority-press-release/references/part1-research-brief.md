# Part 1: Authority Stack Research Brief

Role: expert real estate PR strategist, research analyst, and news-style positioning specialist. Produce a factual research brief that Part 2 will use as its single source of truth.

## Research Rules

- Use only factual, source-backed information found through live web research (WebSearch / web_fetch).
- - Do not invent claims, awards, project history, pricing, timelines, incentives, availability, affiliations, partnerships, or quotes.
  - - Pull quotes only from official websites, press releases, public interviews, public project pages, municipal sources, credible news articles, or trusted public records.
    - - Log every source as you go - title, URL, and the exact claim it supports. A claim without a source gets cut or flagged, never kept on faith.
      - - Position the agent as a buyer-side local specialist unless there is clear proof of official affiliation.
       
        - ## Research Steps
       
        - **Step 1 - Core project facts.** Project name, address, city, neighborhood, product type, price range (if public), residence count, floor plans, square footage, amenities, developer, builder, design team, sales team, delivery status, and the official project description.
       
        - **Step 2 - Identify authority players.** Every credible party connected to the project: developer, builder, architect, interior designer, brokerage, sales team, land planner, brand partner, wellness partner, hospitality partner, amenity partner, municipal partner, others named publicly.
       
        - **Step 3 - Player credibility notes.** For each player: company background, relevant past projects, awards or recognition (only if sourced), design/building philosophy, notable public quotes, why this player matters to buyers, and the source for every claim.
       
        - **Step 4 - Local proof.** Location facts that strengthen the campaign: parks, beaches, trails, downtown areas, dining, shopping, schools (when relevant), employment centers, hospitals, cultural venues, transit, road improvements, municipal plans, nearby development, neighborhood growth, lifestyle anchors. Explain why each point matters to the likely buyer.
       
        - **Step 5 - Use the avatar.** From any buyer avatar, ICA, or audience research documents in the project folder (auto-loaded) summarize: buyer fears, lifestyle desires, status signals, practical concerns, objections, location needs, what they value most, and what would make this project feel credible, timely, or worth touring. Use this to recommend which facts, players, and local proof to emphasize in Part 2.
       
        - **Step 6 - Match project type.**
        - - Luxury: prioritize architecture, interiors, brand, scarcity, hospitality, wellness, design, privacy, views, cultural identity, service, lifestyle, long-term prestige.
          - - Regular community: prioritize builder reputation, value, location, schools, warranty, incentives, commute, move-in timing, amenities, buyer fit, affordability vs nearby options.
           
            - **Step 7 - Compliance review.** Flag risky claims. Do not imply the agent is the official project representative unless proven. Do not imply endorsement, exclusivity, preferred status, or guaranteed pricing/incentives/returns/availability.
           
            - ## Output Format
           
            - Document titled `Authority Stack Research Brief: [Project Name]`, saved as `[Project]_Authority_Research_Brief.docx` in the property's Press Releases folder. Sections:
           
            - 1. Project Snapshot
              2. 2. Buyer Avatar Summary (if provided)
                 3. 3. Authority Stack Overview
                    4. 4. Player Backgrounds and Why They Matter
                       5. 5. Public Pull-Quote Bank From Sources
                          6. 6. Local Proof and Buyer Relevance
                             7. 7. Recommended Buyer Angles
                                8. 8. Possible "Wait, What?" Story Tensions
                                   9. 9. Compliance Notes
                                      10. 10. Source List With Claim Notes
                                         
                                          11. Source list format for each source: Source Title, URL, Used For (the exact claim, quote, player background, project fact, local proof, or history it supports).
                                         
                                          12. ## Final Instruction
                                         
                                          13. Do not write press releases in Part 1. End with: "Part 1 complete. This Authority Stack Research Brief is saved and ready as the required input for Part 2." Then offer to run Part 2.
